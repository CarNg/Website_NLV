Aracde
Arcade is a small scene developed in Unity as part of a Games Programming assignment. The idea was to demonstrate what we learned in C# for Unity. We were asked to demonstrate a number of different skills. These skills included: using various game object types, event handling, appropriate use of physics and audio content, Mecanim for animation, retention and use of state variables and finally, design and implementation of a HUD, game controls and some form of AI.

Why 'Arcade'?
Arcade was developed from a programming aspect rather than game design. So the goal was to develop a small scene that would demonstrate a number of features. As a result, there are two parts to Arcade. There's the opening scene where the player controls a character around an aracde. This scene demonstrates a number of things such as Mecanim animation in all the characters, including NPCs and AI the arcade employee. Arcade was also used as an opportunity to refine Turntable. Here, I was able to add saving features to the game as well as refine some controls. By incorporating Turntable into the game, I was also able to introduce saving with the coins and tickets system in the game.

Gameplay
The game involves the player in an arcade where they can play Turntable where they will be able to win tickets dependent on the score they get in the game. These tickets can then be exchanged for coins in order to play more games of Turntable. In order to do this, the player simply has to go up to the desk where the employee will exchange them. Project Role
Arcade was a solo project. I can take credit for the programming and game design of the game. However, all models, audio and art work is not mine. If there are any issues regarding this, please contact me and I will make amends immediately.

Results and Experience
Arcade was a great exercise for myself to become familiar with C# and Unity. It allowed me to work with and learn a number of different functions and aspects of programming. Many of the things I learned from working in Arcade was later applied and expanded upon in different projects. Although, many of the things in Arcade were simple and the basics, it helped significantly in understanding the basics allowing me to further expand and develop on them for later uses in future games.
